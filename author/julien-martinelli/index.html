Biography

I am a postdoc at Inria, in the SISTM team, and a member of the ELLIS AI network. I work on the interplay between data-driven and mechanistic models for Systems Biology in a Bayesian framework, a field I call Biology-Informed Machine Learning.

Before that, I was a postdoc at Aalto University, in the Probabilistic Machine Learning group, working with Samuel Kaski, Vikas Garg and Markus Heinonen. I was working on Bayesian Inference, Bayesian Optimization, and the integration of human feedback into ML algorithms, with an emphasis on Drug Design.

And prior to that, I did my PhD at Inria and Institut Curie, under the supervision of FranÃ§ois Fages and Annabelle Ballesta. We developed methods at the boundary of Systems Biology and Machine Learning to infer mechanistic models from time series data. A major contribution of the manuscript was to apply such methods to investigate the impact of systemic regulators (e.g. temperature, nutrient exposure, hormones) on the cellular circadian clock, in an attempt to personalize Cancer Chronotherapies. The manuscript can be found here and the slides here.
